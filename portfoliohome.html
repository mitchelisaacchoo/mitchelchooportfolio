Mitchel Choo is a Singaporean photographer and videographer currently based in Sherborne, Dorset, England, working at the intersection of visual storytelling and artistic composition. With a background shaped by a fascination for preserving fleeting moments, his work reflects a deliberate effort to turn the everyday into something lasting. Over the past two years, Mitchel has developed a freelance practice that moves fluidly between photography and film, guided by an instinct for framing, rhythm, and atmosphere. Whether behind the lens on the street or crafting visuals for emerging brands, his approach remains grounded in intention and curiosity.
His work spans street, fashion, travel, and events, with a distinct focus on portraiture—capturing presence in a way that feels both intimate and expressive. Through carefully observed details and subtle emotion, Mitchel creates portraits that reveal character, whether in the quiet gaze of a subject or the natural behavior of an animal. Each frame is approached with patience and intention, allowing authenticity to emerge while maintaining a cinematic sensibility. By working primarily with natural light, he preserves organic textures and tones, shaping portraits that feel honest, immersive, and deeply personal.
Blending moody tones with vibrant contrasts, Mitchel develops a cinematic visual language that carries across both photography and videography. His approach emphasizes atmosphere and depth, using light and shadow to construct scenes that feel immersive and visually striking. While photography leans into natural light for authenticity, his cinematography incorporates controlled artificial lighting to refine mood and enhance storytelling. Across personal and narrative-driven projects, he experiments with tone, pacing, and composition to build visuals that feel both contemporary and emotionally resonant.
Creativity sits at the core of Mitchel’s work, shaped in part by his mild deuteranopia (red-green colourblindness), which informs a unique approach to colour and composition. Rather than limiting his palette, this perspective drives him to explore bold, vibrant tones and unconventional colour pairings, resulting in a distinct and recognizable colour science. His artistic process embraces experimentation and intuition, pushing beyond conventional aesthetics to create imagery that feels fresh, dynamic, and intentional. Each project becomes an opportunity to reinterpret colour and mood, offering a visual identity that stands apart through both limitation and innovation.
Alongside his personal work, Mitchel collaborates with brands and creative teams to translate ideas into visually compelling content. As a social media videographer, he contributes a contemporary visual language tailored for digital platforms, balancing storytelling with strong visual identity. He remains adaptable across projects of varying scales, from structured campaigns to more intimate collaborations, always focusing on clarity, detail, and impact. Whether working with established brands or emerging creatives, Mitchel approaches each collaboration with a commitment to crafting thoughtful, engaging visuals that align with a shared vision.
